### 📝 How to Match an Employee to a Classification

Moving up the levels is based on the actual duties performed and the level of supervision required, not just time served .

- **FLH1 (Entry-Level):** A new starter. Works under **direct supervision** on routine tasks like basic mustering, fence repairs, and packing .
- **FLH2 to FLH4 (Developing):** Gaining experience. Works under **routine supervision**. Takes on more responsibility like operating machinery (e.g., tractor, forklift), assisting tradespeople, and basic stock sorting .
- **FLH5 to FLH6 (Experienced/Skilled):** Competent and autonomous. Holds or is working towards a relevant Certificate III. Performs complex tasks with **minimal supervision**, manages livestock health, and uses chemicals .
- **FLH7 to FLH8 (Senior/Leading Hand):** High-level autonomy and coordination. **Guides daily operations**, solves problems, and may supervise or train other staff members .

### 💡 Special Notes for Dairy Farmers

If you are specifically in the dairy industry, the award links these standard FLH levels to specific dairy job titles to make classification easier :

- **FLH1**: Dairy Operator Grade 1A
- **FLH3**: Dairy Operator Grade 1B
- **FLH5**: Dairy Operator Grade 2
- **FLH7**: Senior Dairy Operator Grade 1
- **FLH8**: Senior Dairy Operator Grade 2

This means a "Senior Dairy Operator" must be paid at least the FLH7 rate of $28.12 per hour (or higher if they meet the FLH8 criteria) .
