### ⚖️ How to Determine the Correct Pay Rate

As a 17-year-old in the farming industry, your minimum pay rate depends on the specific Award or Agreement that covers your job and your level of experience.

*   **Check the Pastoral Award 2020 (Most Likely)** : This is the main award for employees in the pastoral industry, which includes beef and dairy farming . Your pay is based on a **classification level** (e.g., Level 1, Level 2) for a "Farm and Livestock Hand." For junior employees, the base adult rate is reduced by a set percentage based on age. **Since the search results don't provide the specific percentage for 17-year-olds, you must verify this directly.**
*   **Check Your Employment Agreement**: If you have a contract or are covered by a specific Enterprise Agreement, those rates will apply as long as they are higher than the Award minimum.
*   **How to Find Accurate Numbers**:
    *   **Use the Pay Calculator**: The Fair Work Ombudsman website has a "Pay and Conditions Tool" (PACT). You can enter your age, industry, and job role to get the exact minimum hourly rate.
    *   **Call the Fair Work Ombudsman**: They can provide free, confidential advice specific to your situation.

### 📅 Do Public Holidays Apply to You?

Yes, public holidays do apply to you. However, because the farming industry has unique operational needs, there are special rules:

*   **General Rule**: You are entitled to be absent from work on a public holiday and be paid your base rate for that day.
*   **Farming Exception**: The Pastoral Award allows employers to **require you to work on a public holiday** if it is considered **reasonable** given the operational needs of a farm (e.g., milking, feeding, or harvesting cannot stop) .
*   **Payment for Working**: If you are required to work on a public holiday, you must be paid at **penalty rates** (e.g., double time or 2.5x your base rate), not your ordinary rate.
*   **Action**: Your employer should provide a written agreement or policy explaining which public holidays they expect you to work. If they ask you to work without a clear policy, ask for it in writing.

### ⏰ Special Rules for Weekends and Breaks

You are entitled to specific rules regarding weekend work and rest breaks. These rules are in place to protect your health and safety.

**1. Breaks (Meal Breaks)**
*   **Right to a Break**: You have the right to a meal break, usually 30-60 minutes, for every 5 hours of work .
*   **Working Without a Break**: The employer **cannot force you to work for more than 5 hours without a break**. If you do, they must pay you at **overtime rates** until you get your break. You also have the right to take the break later.
*   **Action**: If you're asked to skip a break, say: "I need to take my meal break as required by the Award."

**2. Weekend Work**
*   **Saturday**: Working on a Saturday usually attracts a higher penalty rate (e.g., time and a half or double time), though the exact rate depends on the specific Award.
*   **Sunday**: Working on a Sunday generally has an even higher penalty rate (e.g., double time).
*   **Action**: Check your payslip to ensure you are being paid the weekend penalty rate. If not, ask your employer for a breakdown of how they calculate your weekend pay.

### 🛠️ Your Next Steps

1.  **Confirm Your Award**: Ask your employer directly: "Which Award or Agreement covers my position?"
2.  **Find Your Pay Rate**: Use the Fair Work Ombudsman's **Pay and Conditions Tool (PACT)** online. Enter "Pastoral Award 2020," your location, and your age (17). It will calculate the exact junior rate.
3.  **Document Everything**: Keep a log of your hours (start, finish, breaks) and your payslips. This is your proof if a dispute arises.
4.  **Contact a Professional**: If your employer refuses to pay you correctly for weekends, public holidays, or refuses to give you breaks, contact the **Fair Work Ombudsman (13 13 94)** for free advice and help.
