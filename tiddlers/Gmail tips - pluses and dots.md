You're referring to Gmail's **plus addressing** feature—a useful built-in trick that lets you create unlimited email aliases without setting up new accounts.

## How the Plus Sign Trick Works

If your email address is **`yourname@gmail.com`**, you can add a `+` sign followed by any text before the `@` symbol:

- `yourname+shopping@gmail.com`
- `yourname+newsletters@gmail.com`
- `yourname+work@gmail.com`
- `yourname+anythingyouwant@gmail.com`

All emails sent to these variations still arrive in your main `yourname@gmail.com` inbox.

## What You Can Use It For

| Use Case | Example | Benefit |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Tracking data leaks** | `yourname+vizio@gmail.com` | If spam arrives at this address, you know Vizio sold/shared your data |
| **Auto-filtering** | `yourname+bank@gmail.com` | Create filters that label or sort these emails automatically |
| **Reducing spam** | `yourname+newsletter@gmail.com` | Filter emails to this alias straight to trash if spam starts coming in |
| **Organization** | `yourname+receipts@gmail.com` | All receipts go to one labeled folder |

## Important Limitations

**Not all websites accept it** — Some online forms reject the `+` sign as an invalid character, kicking back an error message. In my experience, most modern sites accept it, but banks and older systems can be problematic.

**Spammers may strip it** — Some spammers have caught on and automatically remove anything after the `+` before selling or using your address.

**Cannot send from aliases** — These `+` addresses work for *receiving* email, but you typically can't send email *from* them without additional setup (though you can configure Gmail to send as an alias).

## The Dot Trick (Bonus)

Gmail also ignores periods in the username part of your address. `first.last@gmail.com`, `firstlast@gmail.com`, and `f.i.r.s.t.l.a.s.t@gmail.com` all go to the same inbox. Unlike the plus sign, dots are almost never rejected by website forms.

## Filtering Setup

Once you start using aliases, go to **Gmail Settings → Filters and Blocked Addresses** to create rules. For example, any email sent to `+shopping` can be automatically labeled "Shopping," marked as read, or sent to a specific folder.
