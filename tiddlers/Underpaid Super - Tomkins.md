Here's a practical guide on how to handle this situation in Australia:

## Paying Back Superannuation (Super Catch-Up for Employers)

### Step 1 – Work Out What's Owed

Calculate the shortfall for each quarter the payments were missed or underpaid. The current Super Guarantee (SG) rate is **11.5% of ordinary time earnings** (rising to 12% from 1 July 2025). Include all affected pay periods going back over the year+.

---

### Step 2 – Pay the Super Guarantee Charge (SGC) via the ATO

Because the super wasn't paid **on time**, you can't just back-pay directly to the fund and be compliant. Instead, you must go through the ATO:

1. **Lodge a Super Guarantee Charge Statement** for each quarter you missed, using the ATO's online form (via Online Services for Business or your tax agent).
2. The SGC is calculated on a broader earnings base than regular SG (it includes things like some allowances that normal SG doesn't).
3. The SGC amount includes:
   - The unpaid super shortfall
   - **Interest of 10% p.a.** on the shortfall
   - An **administration fee of $20 per employee per quarter**

---

### Step 3 – Pay the SGC to the ATO

The ATO then distributes the employee's entitlement directly to their super fund. You cannot bypass this process if the deadlines have passed.

---

### Step 4 – Consider Voluntary Disclosure

If you're coming forward proactively (before the ATO contacts you), the ATO's **Voluntary Disclosure** process can reduce penalties. Penalties on top of SGC can be up to **200% of the SGC amount**, but voluntary disclosure typically brings this down significantly.

> Lodge via: ATO Online Services for Business → "Super" → "Unpaid super"

---

### Important Things to Know

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **SGC due dates** | SGC statements are due 1 month after the quarter's SG deadline |
| **Tax deductibility** | SGC itself is **not tax-deductible** (unlike normal SG contributions) |
| **Employee notification** | You should notify the employee of the corrective action |
| **Statute of limitations** | The ATO can go back many years on unpaid super |

---

### Options if You Can't Pay in Full

The ATO does offer **payment plans** for SGC debts — contact them on **13 10 20** (business line) or via your tax agent to arrange this.

---

### Recommended Next Steps

1. Engage a **tax agent or accountant** to calculate the exact SGC for each quarter — the earnings base difference can catch people out.
2. Lodge the SGC statements as soon as possible to stop interest accumulating.
3. Consider getting professional advice if the amounts are significant, as penalties can escalate quickly.
